About Me

Currently a sophomore at Olin College of Engineering, I am passionate about making, photography, and flight in all its forms. I grew up on a farm in Missouri, building and flying model airplanes over our horses and sheep. During my gap year in 2017, I became a commercial UAS pilot, learned welding and composites, and taught in a makerspace. In 2019, I received my pilot’s license.

At Olin, I am the structures and composites lead for Olin Rocketry, participate in aerial robotics research, assist the Design Build Fly team, and sing in our a-cappella group, the PowerChords.
